The term has been extended to works including sound, text and light. #### TYPES OF SCULPTURE - **Subtractive process** Subtractive sculpture is the oldest form of sculpture and involves removing material, as in wood carving or stone sculpture, to create a finished work. Subtractive sculpture is by far the most technically difficult and due to the nature of the medium is the most restrictive in expression. - **Additive process** Additive sculpture describes all other forms of sculpture and the process most commonly used today. Simply put, additive sculpture is the process of creating sculpture by adding material to create the work. #### WAYS TO CONSTRUCT - **Carving** Subtractive process: material is removed: mainly wood and stone (marble). - **Modeling** Additive process: material is added: clay, wax, plaster, paper-mache. - **Casting** Casting: a mold is used to form molten bronze (or other material) into a desired shape. #### MORE WAYS TO CONSTRUCT - **Attachment**: welding, gluing, nailing materials together. - **Assemblage**: assembling found objects in unique ways. - **Relief**: attached to a surface. - **Kinetic Sculpture**: movable parts (wind). #### OVERVIEW - [조소란 무엇인가?-용문중학교 미술교실](http://www.artplus.or.kr/pdf_doc/sculpture.pdf) - Painting and sculpture: let's compare to [Wassily Kandinsky's Point and line to plane ](http://www.saylor.org/site/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Wassily-Kandinsky.pdf) - Full text of [Point and line to plane : contribution to the analysis of the pictorial elements](https://archive.org/stream/pointlinetoplane00kand#page/25/mode/1up) ---------------------- ## Spatial Dynamics I ### Dimensions ![ndimen](img/ndimen02.png) - Image from [Cubes: n-dimensional.de](http://n-dimensional.de/cubes/) ### Fractional dimension: recursive process ![ndimen](img/ndimen01_01.png) ![ndimen](img/ndimen01.jpg) - Hilbert cubes: Hilbert Curve in 3d (levels 1 to 6). - Image from [Through the Interface](http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2008/07/turtle-fractals.html) ![ndimen](img/ndimen_novak.png) - Transvergence class note. ### References [The Algorithmic Beauty of Plants](http://algorithmicbotany.org/papers/#abop) This book was originally printed by Springer-Verlag in 1990 (second printing 1996).
