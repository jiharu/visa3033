This happens due to: - Increasing democratization of manufacturing. - Influx of consumers turning to casual product designs. - The situation is similar to the invention of the printing press, the widespread affordability of cameras, the advent of MP3 file formats and other forms of online file sharing. ## Two cases ### Low poly Final Fantasy VII models by Joaquin Baldwin (director and 3D animator) - In July 2013, Joaquin Baldwin, a Walt Disney Animation Studio digital cinematographer, sent low polygon 3D models taken from Final Fantasy VII to Shapeways which didn't exist at the time. Shapeways offers not only a print service but artists the option to sell their designs in an online store so that the company can print and ship orders on demand. - [Joaquin Baldwin Shapeways shop](http://www.shapeways.com/shops/joabaldwin?s=0#more-products) - [Who wants 3D printed Final Fantasy VII figures?
