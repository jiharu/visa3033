Wax prototyped translated into hydrocal casting. ## 3D printing types ### Conventional construction methods - **Subtractive process** Subtractive sculpture is the oldest form of sculpture and involves removing material, as in wood carving or stone sculpture, to create a finished work. Subtractive sculpture is by far the most technically difficult and due to the nature of the medium is the most restrictive in expression: carving. - **Additive process** Additive sculpture describes all other forms of sculpture and the process most commonly used today. Simply put, additive sculpture is the process of creating sculpture by adding material to create the work: modeling with clay, wax, plaster, and paper-maché. - Casting: a mold is used to form molten material into a desired shape. #### Assembly and attachment - Attachment: welding, gluing, nailing materials together. - Assembly: the act of connecting together the parts of something such as a machine. ## 3D printing methods - Plastic Jet Printing Process: also called Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), is the simplest and lowest cost method but limited to plastics and soft metals. Also lower precision. Thermoplastic materials such as ABS and polycarbonates of various types. Some applications use soft metals. Material is fed into heated nozzle from a spool of thread or wire. - Stereolithography (SLA): Photosensitive resins of various types, clear or colored, in liquid form, poured into printing machine container. Material is hardened by ultraviolet light from laser. Remaining resin is reused. - Selective Laser Sintering (SLS): Powders of polymers such as nylon (plain, glass-filled, or with other fillers) or polystyrene - metals including steel, titanium, gold, silver, alloy mixtures – ceramics of various kinds. High energy laser beam sinters (melts) the powder to form layers and attaching layers to each other.
