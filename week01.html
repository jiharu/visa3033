However strictly speaking, additive manufacture is different from most other traditional manufacturing methods. - Casting: a mold is used to form molten material into a desired shape. - **Subtractive process** Subtractive sculpture is the oldest form of sculpture and involves removing material, as in wood carving or stone sculpture, to create a finished work. Subtractive sculpture is by far the most technically difficult and due to the nature of the medium is the most restrictive in expression: carving, milling, and cutting. - Other construction methods - Attachment: welding, gluing, sawing, nailing materials together. - Assembly: the act of connecting together the parts of something such as a machine. - The Ten Principles of 3D Printing: An excerpt from Hod Lipson and Melba Kurman's new book Fabricated: The New World of 3D Printing [big think blog link](http://bigthink.com/experts-corner/the-ten-principles-of-3d-printing). ### Introductional video - [Animation film: Fully printed - a vision for the future of 3D printing by the Disseny Hub museum and gallery in Barcelona, 2010 [4:27]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Eppr1erkI3A) ### Introductional TED video - [Ted talk Lisa Harouni: A primer on 3D printing, 2011 [14:49]](https://www.ted.com/talks/lisa_harouni_a_primer_on_3d_printing?language=en#t-35346) ## History ### Photosculpture - **1860:** A revolving series of 24 photographs was reconstructed into a three-dimensional sculpture. Digital photograph of stereolithographic objects. "In 1982, I had dived into computer graphics field as a art director with 3 technical people. After spending 4 years for generating moving images on video tape, I had felt frustration with the computer images, because the image was generated with 3D data but we can not touch that 3D object. I had had strong desire to materialize the object which is from computer data, for touching the surface. The results were shown as computer generated sculpture in 1987 titled Geometric Love, NC milling machine was used for realization and in 1990 titled Forbidden Fruits, Stereo lithography technology was used." [from Fujihata's artist statement.](http://www.iamas.ac.jp/interaction/i97/artist_Fujihata.html) ![week1_2](img/collins.gif) Dan Collins, 'Of More Than Two Minds', hydrocal casting, 1993. This sculpture was produced by capturing the movement of the artist using 3D laser scanning. The scanned data was then translated to a CAD model that was subsequently milled in wax using CNC milling. Wax prototyped translated into hydrocal casting. ## 3D printing methods ### Binding process Printing objects by using a printhead to bind a pre-spread layer of material, each time tracing and binding the cross sections of the object. - Stereolithography (SLA): Photosensitive resins of various types, clear or colored, in liquid form, poured into printing machine container. Material is hardened by ultraviolet light from laser. Remaining resin is reused. - Selective Laser Sintering (SLS): Powders of polymers such as nylon (plain, glass-filled, or with other fillers) or polystyrene - metals including steel, titanium, gold, silver, alloy mixtures – ceramics of various kinds. High energy laser beam sinters (melts) the powder to form layers and attaching layers to each other. Unsintered powder serves as support for object until completed, then is reused. - Inkjet Powder Printing (3DP) - Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM) ### Deposition Processes Using a printhead to deposit the material itself on the previous section of extruded material. - Fused Deposition Modeling [FDM]: Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) is an additive manufacturing technology that builds parts up layer-by-layer by heating and extruding thermoplastic filament. - Polyjet >- [Stratasys video](http://www.stratasys.com/resources/3d-printing-videos) Plastic Jet Printing Process: also called Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), is the simplest and lowest cost method but limited to plastics and soft metals. Also lower precision. Thermoplastic materials such as ABS and polycarbonates of various types. Some applications use soft metals. Material is fed into heated nozzle from a spool of thread or wire. - Paste Extrusion - [3D pen: 3Doodler PRO](http://3doodlerpro.com/) - A group of University of Tokyo students overseen by architect Kengo Kuma have developed a 3D-printing pen that can be used to create complex architectural structures out of plastic sticks: ["drawn-in-place"](http://www.dezeen.com/2016/02/22/tokyo-university-research-students-3d-printed-pen-complex-architectural-structures-plastic/) - 3D-printed chair by The Barlett UCL students: [The Spatial Curves project](http://www.dezeen.com/2016/02/05/bartlett-students-ucl-3d-printed-filigree-chairs-robots/) - [Lyx 3D pen](http://www.dezeen.com/2014/05/01/lix-smallest-3d-printing-pen-aluminium/) ---------------------- ## Case study ### Nervous system "**Nervous System** is a generative design studio that works at the intersection of science, art, and technology.
